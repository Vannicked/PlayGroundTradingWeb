Stocks are like owning a tiny piece of a company. When you buy a stock, you become a partial owner, and you might get some money back if the company does well.

Bonds are a bit like lending money. When you buy a bond, you're giving a loan to a company or a government. They promise to pay you back the loan amount (principal) with a little extra (interest) over time.

ETFs are like a collection of different things, such as stocks or bonds, bundled together. It's like buying a variety pack that helps you invest in many things at once.

Mutual funds are a way for many people to put their money together to invest in lots of different things, like stocks and bonds. Professionals manage these funds to help them grow.

Futures are like making a deal today to buy or sell something in the future at a set price. It's a bit like agreeing to buy a video game when it comes out, but at today's price.

Options are a bit like having a special ticket that lets you choose to buy or sell something in the future. It's a choice, not a must-do. It's like getting the option to buy a concert ticket later if you decide you want it.

Trading is like buying and selling concert tickets quickly for short-term profit. Investing is like buying a season pass for a concert series, aiming for long-term growth.
